## 📋 Executive Summary (BLUF)
This repository contains the data, modeling scripts, and strategic analysis for the **March 2026 Kinetic-to-Compute Transition**. Following the regional escalation during **Operation Epic Fury** (CSIS, Feb 2026), a series of kinetic drone strikes targeted Tier-1 data center clusters in Abu Dhabi and Riyadh between March 1–3, 2026. 

This model quantifies the systemic contagion caused by digital infrastructure disruption, moving beyond traditional "Hydrocarbon Hegemony" to the new era of **Compute Sovereignty**. Key findings indicate that proximity to strike zones in the Gulf correlates with an acute evaporation of digital capital, with estimated 72-hour downtime costs exceeding **$104M USD** for major financial institutions (Emirates NBD, ADCB).

## 🧮 Mathematical Foundation

### 1. OLS Regression Model
We demonstrate the correlation between kinetic strike proximity ($d$) and market volatility ($\sigma$) on the Tadawul (TASI):

$$
\sigma = \beta_0 + \beta_1 d + \epsilon
$$

*   **Key Performance Indicator (KPI):** $R^2 = 0.9416$ (demonstrating that proximity to compute nodes is now a primary driver of regional market volatility).

### 2. COCO Framework (Cost of Data Center Outage)
Quantifying the cumulative capital evaporation during the 72-hour AWS outage:

$$
C_{total} = \int_{0}^{T} (R_h \cdot \alpha + P_h \cdot \beta) dt
$$

Where $R_h$ is hourly revenue loss, $P_h$ is productivity loss, and $\alpha, \beta$ are sector-specific volatility coefficients.
